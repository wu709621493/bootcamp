# Forgo vs. Fargo

| Term | Part of speech | Meaning | Example usage |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **forgo** (also spelled *forego*, less common) | Verb | To do without or abstain from something. | "She chose to forgo dessert." |
| **Fargo** | Proper noun | A city in North Dakota, USA; also the title of a film and television series named after the city. | "They flew to Fargo for the festival." |

## When to use each
- Use **forgo** when you mean skipping, giving up, or deciding not to have something.
- Use **Fargo** when referring to the city or the works titled after it (the Coen brothers' 1996 film or the anthology TV series).

## Tips to remember
- **forgo** contains **go**, hinting at "letting go" of something.
- **Fargo** starts with **Far-**, matching the place name: *Fargo, North Dakota*.
